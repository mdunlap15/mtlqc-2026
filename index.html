Before you go

Two short flights, one document check for Yulia, and the little logistics that make the rest easy.

Flights

Both booked: American 4550 out (LGA 8:19 AM → YUL 9:54 AM, Sun Jul 19) and Delta 5449 back (YUL 7:44 PM → LGA 9:20 PM, Fri Jul 24) — an early arrival and an evening return, so you get full days on both ends. It's an international hop, so both of you need passports — full details live in the Bookings tab.

From YUL to Mile End is 20–30 minutes: a taxi/Uber runs roughly $45 CAD, or the 747 airport bus feeds the metro if you're traveling light.

Yulia's docs
green card + Russian passport

Carry the valid green card and the valid Russian passport together — the border officer will want both. Under the current federal guidance, lawful permanent residents of the U.S. are exempt from the electronic travel authorization (eTA) when flying to Canada, and just need proof of status plus a passport from their country of nationality.

Rules on this have shifted before, so double-check on canada.ca a week or two out. If there's any doubt, an eTA is a five-minute online form and about CAD $7 — cheap insurance, worth doing rather than risking the gate.

Your week

Just for Laughs is on all week

Good timing. The festival runs Jul 15–26, so it covers your whole stay — the Quartier des Spectacles downtown becomes the comedy capital of the world, with ticketed headliners indoors and plenty of free outdoor shows and street performers most evenings. Grab tickets early if there's a name you want.

Good to know

Money: Canadian dollars; cards work everywhere; tip 15–20%. Language: French first — a "bonjour / merci" goes a long way, and everyone flips to English easily. Getting around: Mile End is walkable, the metro is quick (Laurier & Rosemont stations), and BIXI bikes are all over. You'll barely need a car — the train handles Québec City (see Wednesday).

01Sunday · Jul 19

Land & settle into Mile End

Day one has one fixed point — the World Cup final at 3 PM. Land around 10, stash the bags (check-in isn't until 4), ease in with a coffee and a warm bagel, then go find a crowd for the match.

Late morning
stash the bags

Sam can't do an early check-in, so the easy move is to drop the bags rather than haul them for six hours. Several luggage-storage points sit right in Mile End — reserve one through Bounce, LuggageHero, or Radical Storage (~$6–8 CAD per bag, tucked inside host cafés and shops, booked in the app), then collect them on your way in before 4 PM. Montréal's train station has no lockers, so the app networks are the way to go.

Rather keep the bags with you? The comfortable, bag-friendly play is a long welcome brunch on a terrasse plus the open-air Jean-Talon Market — and save the Mont Royal climb for Monday.

Up the river to Québec City

You're doing this properly — two nights, not a rushed day trip. A relaxed ride up, an afternoon in the old city, and the cobblestones to yourselves once the day-trippers clear out. Québec gets all of Thursday, too.

The train · booked

An early ride up, into the old town by noon

Québec City is about three hours by VIA Rail, straight into Gare du Palais in the heart of the old town — no transfers, no airport shuffle. Your 8:36 departure means an early check-out from the Airbnb (leave by ~8), but the reward is a full first afternoon in Québec. Sit on the right for river glimpses.
